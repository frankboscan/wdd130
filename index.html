My name is Frank Boscan. I enjoy classic literature and doing the dishes. Or at least, I love having a clean kitchen and clean dishes. My favorite novel is Dumas' The Three Musketeers. I am such a fan that my first two sons' middle names are respectively Athos and Porthos. And the next one shall be Aramis, and the fourth shall be D'Artagnan. I am ever so thankful my wife didn't object to those choices, although she is worried that she won't get a girl. For the record, I have nothing against girls, but I do need my musketeers!

I also am a big fan of high-fidelity music. An awesome former employer paid for some high-fidelity speakers a few years ago. I hadn't ever really paid attentiont to the sound quality of my music before, but after listening for a few seconds, I was converted and cannot listen to anything less anymore. This led to my subsequent purchase of hi-fi headphones (well-worth it). My last birthday gift was a digital-analog converter (DAC), which improves audio quality coming from a computer or other digital device. Now, I don't have to settle for the basic sound-quality produced by my laptop; I can connect to my speaker system or plug in my DAC and hi-fi headphones and listen to great audio. I highly recommend this to anyone who hasn't tested hi-fi, in case it isn't obvious. Join the audiophile club!

France

Many of the touristic areas of France have remained stuck in the Medieval Age, and this has been deliberate governmental policy so as to preserve the nation's cultural heritage. However, much of the rest of the nation is also a museum, although depicting a more recent history. My city, Maromme, for example, has many homes that were built during the pre-WW2 period and have been only lightly modernized since (some insulation, plumbing, electricity, etc. have been added). But the architecture and street layout remains much the same. The roads, not originally designed for cars, are one-way in many areas. The common brick homes in this valley reflect the strong masonry influences from the period of industrialization.
