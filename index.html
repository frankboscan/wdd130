France

Many of the touristic areas of France have remained stuck in the Medieval Age, and this has been deliberate governmental policy so as to preserve the nation's cultural heritage. However, much of the rest of the nation is also a museum, although depicting a more recent history. My city, Maromme, for example, has many homes that were built during the pre-WW2 period and have been only lightly modernized since (some insulation, plumbing, electricity, etc. have been added). But the architecture and street layout remains much the same. The roads, not originally designed for cars, are one-way in many areas. The common brick homes in this valley reflect the strong masonry influences from the period of industrialization.
